Sinigang

Sinigang is a dish originated in the Philippines. Typically, it is composed of fish, meat, vegetables, tomatoes and tamarind flavoring. The reason why it is called “sinigang” is that it was a translation to the English word, “stew” which is true because of its method of cooking.

Adobo

The word adobo is derived from the Spanish word adobar, which means “marinade” or “pickling sauce.” The existence of the tangy dish was first recorded in 1613 by the Spaniard Pedro de San Buenaventura.

Sisig

The dish's name comes from “sisigan,” an old Tagalog word which means “to make it sour.” Its existence was first recorded in a Kapampangan dictionary back in 1732 by Diego Bergaño, a Spanish missionary who served as the parish priest for Mexico, Pampanga at the time
